# Aquarium System

---

## Overview

The aquarium is the heart of Axolittle’s home screen and a living environment that mechanically affects gameplay, not just cosmetic. Tank quality directly impacts axolotl wellbeing. Players manage water quality, filter upgrades, and decorations as ongoing care tasks alongside feeding and play.

---

## Home Screen

The home screen is portrait orientation, displayed as an underwater aquarium view. The axolotl lives in the center of the screen. Decorations, and environmental elements surround it. The tank is the first thing players see every time they open the app — it should feel alive and personal.

---

## Water Quality

Water Quality is a wellbeing stat that decays slowly over real-world time, including when the app is closed. It acts as a passive multiplier on all other wellbeing stats — poor water quality accelerates the decay of Hunger, Happiness, and Cleanliness simultaneously. Good water quality slightly slows their decay.

**Managed through:**

- Water changes — player-initiated task that restores Water Quality directly. Water change locks all mini games for 2 hours while the tank is cleaned.
- Filter tier — higher filters slow the passive decay rate significantly
- See: Wellbeing & Stats for full Water Quality decay rates by life stage

---

## Filter Upgrades

Filters are the primary long-term investment in tank health. Three tiers, each purchased with Bubbles from the shop.

| Tier | Effect | Cost |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Basic Filter | Default. Standard Water Quality decay rate | Coins |
| Advanced Filter | Slows Water Quality decay by ~40% | Coins |
| Premium Filter | Slows Water Quality decay by ~70% | Opals |

Upgrading filters is a meaningful progression goal for players who want to reduce the daily maintenance burden on their tank.

## Shrimp

shrimp are a secondary tank management mechanic that adds a layer of ecosystem thinking to axolotl care.

**How they work:**

- Shrimp help passively maintain Cleanliness when present and will eat poop.
- Purchased in packs from the shop with Opals
- Are slowly eaten by the Axolotls at a rate of eating 10 per day
- keeps hunger wellbeing stat full while present
- used as a vacation style mechanic when a player knows they won't be on the game for a 1-3 days.

## Decorations and Plants

Decorations are both cosmetic and functional. 

**Cosmetic :**
Purely visual. Personalise the tank and build emotional attachment to the space. Premium cosmetic bundles, seasonal items, and themed packs available for Opals.

**Functional :**
Provide small passive Happiness bonuses to the axolotl over time. Not dramatic — enough to feel rewarding without making them mandatory purchases.

**Seasonal and limited-time items:**
Special decorations and plants available for a limited window — Halloween cave set, winter wonderland tank, spring bloom theme etc. Creates urgency and collectibility. Available for Opals only.

**Decoration and Plant placement:**
Players place decorations and plants in the aquarium directly. Decorations sit on the tank floor and background. Future iteration could allow tapping decorations to rearrange them.

## Multiple Aquariums (Future release)

Players can maintain up to four separate aquariums, one per axolotl. Each aquarium is completely independent with its own water quality, filter tier, and decoration set.

**Naming:**
Players name each aquarium separately from the axolotl living in it. Both names are displayed. Personalisation drives attachment.

**Aquarium selector:**
A dedicated screen shows all active aquariums as cards. Each card shows the aquarium name, axolotl name, life stage, and a miniature tank preview. Players tap to visit any aquarium.

**Slot availability:**
Players start with one aquarium slot. Additional slots purchasable with Opals up to the four slot maximum.

## Nursery Slot

The nursery is a reserved slot outside the main four aquarium slots. It holds an unhatched egg and does not consume an aquarium slot.

**How it works:**

- Egg appears in the nursery after rebirth or breeding
- 24-hour incubation period before the egg is ready to hatch
- Player chooses when to hatch — no expiry pressure
- Nursery displays egg with parent information and inherited trait preview
- Only one egg can be incubating in the nursery at a time

**Egg storage:**
Additional eggs beyond the one in the nursery are stored separately. Storage limit of approximately 5–6 eggs. Stored eggs do not expire but develop subtle visual aging over time hinting they are ready to hatch. Additional storage slots available as Opal purchase.

---

## Releasing Axolotls

Releasing is an option to free up an aquarium slot. No currency reward. 

**Release flow:**

1. Player selects axolotl and chooses Release
2. Gentle confirmation screen with soft language — “Set [Name] free?”
3. Player confirms
4. Axolotl swims off into open water in a small farewell animation
5. Aquarium slot becomes available immediately — no cooldown
6. If player does not have any eggs in the nursery a common starter egg is given for free
