# Axolittle Game Design

Axolittle — Game Design Document
Studio: Uoma Labs
Platform: iOS (Android after)
Genre: Virtual pet / life simulation
Built in: HTML/CSS/JavaScript wrapped in native iOS shell

Overview
A Tamagotchi-style mobile game where players raise an axolotl through four life stages in a customizable aquarium. Features real aquarium care mechanics, mini games tied to level progression, a rebirth and lineage system that rewards long term play, and social features including friend comparisons and player breeding.

Life Stages
Four stages with varying care demands and durations. Full lifecycle approximately 30-40 days based on an XP bar.
∙	Baby — Levels 1-9. Heavy feeding and cleaning.
∙	Juvenile — Levels 10-19. Heavy play, semi-heavy feeding and cleaning.
∙	Adult — Levels 20-29. Normal demands. Primary breeding window.
∙	Elder — Levels 30-40. Reduced demands. Player chooses when to initiate rebirth.
See: Wellbeing & Stats for decay rates per stage

Rebirth System
Elder axolotl lays an egg when player chooses to let them rest. Framed as a meaningful goodbye not a transaction. Egg sits in nursery slot for 24 hours before hatching.
See: Wellbeing & Stats for neglect progression

Lineage & Inheritance
Each generation passes color tendencies, stat affinities, and passive trait bonuses to the next. Randomness prevents min-maxing. Generation 3-4 may unlock rare variants unavailable any other way.

Mood System
Axolotl displays visible moods reflecting current wellbeing — happy, sad, hungry, sick, playful, tired. Visual state motivates care beyond stat numbers.

Social & Multiplayer
Friend stat comparison, select mini game competitions, and player breeding planned for future update. Ability to send a daily gift and a “poke” notification. Friends will be able to gift an egg if they have extras.
See: Multiplayer (Future Update)

Currency
Two currency system — Coins (free) and Opals (premium). Free players earn Opals through rewarded ads and achievements at a meaningful rate.
See: Currency & Monetization

Aquarium
Home screen is underwater in a customizable living aquarium. Tank environment mechanically affects wellbeing through water quality, filter upgrades. 
See: Aquarium System
