# Mini Games

---

## Overview

Mini games are played for fun and to gain XP and coins. Exceptional performance occasionally drops Opals. Each game is designed to feel distinct and connect thematically to axolotl life.

---

## General Rules

- All games are accessible from the Play screen at any time
- An Energy Bar is presented at the top of the screen and each time a mini game is played players lose one bar. After the bar is empty XP rewards stop.
- Each game session rewards coins on completion regardless of score
- XP rewards scale with performance — normal, good, and exceptional tiers
- Exceptional performance has a chance to drop Opals in addition to XP gains
- Score thresholds per tier to be defined during testing

## 🪁 Keepey Upey

**Difficulty:** Easy — casual entry point

**Mechanic:**
Keep the axolotl afloat as long as possible. Tap to bounce/lift. Gravity pull it down. Obstacles move across the screen. The longer the player survives the higher the score.

**Difficulty scaling:**
Speed and obstacle frequency increase over time. Early sessions are forgiving to onboard new players.

**Win / lose:**
No traditional win state — survival game. Session ends when the axolotl hits the floor/ceiling or an obstacle. Score is total survival time.

## 🪝 Flappy Fish Hooks

**Difficulty:** Easy-Medium — proven addictive mechanic

**Mechanic:**
Flappy Bird style. Player taps to keep the axolotl swimming upward, releases to let it sink. Avoid fish hooks dangling from above and obstacles in the water column. Gap between hooks narrows and frequency of hooks increases as score increases.

**Difficulty scaling:**
Hook frequency and movement speed increase progressively. Hook gap width narrows at score milestones.

**Win / lose:**
Survival game. Session ends on collision with a hook or boundary. Score is number of hooks successfully passed.

## 🥞 Axolotl Stacker

**Difficulty:** Medium — precision timing required

**Mechanic:**
Items swing back and forth on a pendulum or conveyor. Player taps to drop each item onto the growing stack below. Precise timing lands items cleanly. Poor timing causes overhang or collapse. Stack as high as possible.

**Difficulty scaling:**
Swing speed increases with each successful stack. Items become smaller at higher levels.

**Win / lose:**
Session ends when an item misses the top block entirely. Score is total stack height reached.

---

## ⛏️ Treasure Hunt Cave

**Difficulty:** Medium-Hard 

**Mechanic:**
Axolotl explores a cave system wearing a hard hat. Player navigates through the cave collecting gems and avoiding obstacles — falling rocks, narrow passages, underground creatures. Seed-based course generation means every run has a consistent layout for a given seed.

**Difficulty scaling:**
Caves become more complex with more obstacles and tighter passages at higher score levels. Gem placement becomes sparser requiring more exploration.

**Win / lose:**
Session ends on collision with an obstacle or running out of a stamina/air meter. Score is gems collected plus distance traveled.

---

## 🔢 Math Rush

**Difficulty:** Easy-Medium — accessible but mentally engaging

**Mechanic:**
Equations or number patterns appear on screen. Player selects the correct answer from multiple choice options before a timer runs out. Axolotl-themed framing — counting ghost shrimp, sorting shells, measuring water levels — keeps the math mechanic feeling thematic rather than like homework.

**Difficulty scaling:**
Timer gets shorter at higher levels. Operations progress from addition and subtraction to multiplication, division, and simple pattern recognition.

**Win / lose:**
Session ends when the timer runs out on an unanswered question or a wrong answer is selected. Score is number of correct answers in a row or total correct answers in the session.

**Coral Code**

Difficulty: Selectable Easy, Medium, Hard

Mechanic: Mastermind concept but Axolotl themed: secret code and the player has 10 guesses to get the code.

Win/lose: session ends on successful guessing of the code or after 10 failed guesses.

---

**Fishing**

Difficulty: Easy-Medium

Multiplayer: 2 player- Bot or real person via matchmaking

Mechanic: Player’s axolotl and opponent are on their own boats with fish swimming underneath. Clicking and holding allow the player to drop their line and it continues to go down in the water until they let go or they catch a fish. If a fish is caught they must let go to reel the fish up. Each fish weighs a certain amount and that is tracked on screen.

win/lose: after one minute the player with the most caught weight wins.

---

**Bite Tag**

Difficulty: Medium-Hard

Multiplayer: 4 player- Bots and/or real people via matchmaking

Mechanics: players each start in their own corner and one person is randomly selected to be “it”. The person that is it attempts to tag another player. When a player is tagged they have a bite taken out of them and they become “it”. The previous player who was it is immune from all tags for 3 seconds. 

win/lose: Players who have been bit three times are out. Last player standing or whoever has the least amount of bites at the end of the one minute 30 second timer.

## XP Contribution

All non multiplayer mini game play contributes to the XP bar when played with an energy from the energy bar. Exceptional performance gives bonus XP. This makes mini games the fastest way for an engaged player to progress toward elder transition. For Multiplayer, only winning rewards XP and must have been played from using energy from the Energy bar for XP.
